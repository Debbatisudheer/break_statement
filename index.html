Understanding the "break" Statement

So, "break" helps your program stop doing something when it's done, just like you stop driving when you reach the end of the road. It's a way to say, "Okay, we're finished here, let's move on to the next thing!"

In simple terms, "break" can help speed up your program by skipping unnecessary work, but it doesn't directly use any extra time or space by itself. Its impact depends on how it's used within your program.
